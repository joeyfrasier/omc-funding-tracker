How 4-Way Matching Works

Each invoice (identified by NVC code) is matched across four data legs: 1) Remittance Email from the customer's ERP, 2) Invoice from Worksuite's aggregate DB, 3) Received Payment (MoneyCorp inbound funding from agency), and 4) Outbound Payment (MoneyCorp payment to contractor). Records are joined on NVC code. Amounts are verified within a $0.01 tolerance. The match status reflects how many legs are present.

6 Recommendations

1. Bulk-Mark 303 Historical Records as Funded

All Nov 2025 – Jan 2026 records have both a confirmed invoice and MoneyCorp payment. 93.9% are exact USD matches. The missing remittance email is a documentation gap, not a financial discrepancy. Recommend marking these as "funded" to clear the backlog and focus attention on current-month reconciliation.

2. Increase Remittance Email Coverage

Only 58 of 833 records (7.0%) have remittance data. Two tenants (omcflywheel, omnicomprg) have zero email coverage despite being active. Investigate: Are emails being sent to a different address? Is the email format different from OASYS/D365 ACH? Each new email source could immediately match 50–100+ records.

3. Investigate 18 "Paid" Invoices with No Payment

18 invoices are marked as "Paid" in Worksuite but have no corresponding MoneyCorp payment. These may use a different payment processor, or the MoneyCorp data may be lagging. Concentrated in omnicomprg and omcohg.

4. Add FX-Aware Matching

28 records have valid payments in local currencies (COP, MXN, PHP, GBP, EUR, CNH). These should be matched by comparing against known FX rates or by accepting the MoneyCorp payment as valid regardless of currency conversion differences.
